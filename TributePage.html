Querétaro

Brief History of the City.

Here's a time line of Queretaro's history:

  • 7th to 9th centuries - Indigenous peoples, including the Otomi, Chichimecas, and Tarascans, inhabit the region now known as Querétaro.
  • 15th century - The Purépecha Empire expands into the area, influencing local cultures.
  • 1519 - Spanish explorer Hernán Cortés arrives in the region during his conquest of Mexico.
  • 1531 - The town of Querétaro is founded by Spanish settlers.
  • 1550 - The construction of the aqueduct of Querétaro, an iconic architectural structure, begins.
  • 1655 - The Battle of Cerro de las Campanas takes place, a significant event in the Chichimeca War between indigenous groups and the Spanish.
  • Late 18th century - Querétaro becomes a major center for the production of textiles, pottery, and metals.
  • 1810 - Miguel Hidalgo, a leader of the Mexican War of Independence, issues the Grito de Dolores, which is said to have been shouted in Querétaro.
  • 1821 - Mexico gains independence from Spain.
  • 1867 - The city of Querétaro is the site of the signing of the "Act of Independence of the Mexican North."
  • 1917 - The Mexican Constitution is drafted in Querétaro, making the city known as the "Cradle of the Constitution."
  • 1930 - Querétaro's economic development is boosted with the establishment of the state's first industrial park.
  • 1996 - Querétaro is designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its well-preserved colonial architecture.
  • 2009 - The city of Querétaro is named as the "Historic Monument Zone."
  • 2010 - Querétaro's economy experiences rapid growth, particularly in the aerospace and automotive sectors.
